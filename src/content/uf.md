# university of florida

Gainesville, FL. Go Gators.

I'm studying here while building things on the side. It's a good setup — campus energy, good CS program, close enough to reality that you can build real stuff and test it with real users nearby.

---

## why UF

Florida kid. UF has a strong engineering program, a decent startup scene for a state school, and it's cheap enough that I'm not drowning in debt while trying to build companies.

The irony of building Gradvisr while being a UF student is not lost on me. I am literally the target user.

---

## the balance

Classes, startups, freelance. It's a lot. The key is treating your own projects like a job — not a hobby, not something you do when you have free time (you never have free time), but a scheduled commitment.

I work early mornings before class, late nights when I'm in flow. Weekends go deep on whatever needs the most attention.

---

## what I'm learning

Formally: CS curriculum, math, the usual.  
Actually: how to ship, how to talk to customers, how to scope projects, how to say no to bad ideas fast.

The classroom teaches you to think. Building teaches you everything else.
